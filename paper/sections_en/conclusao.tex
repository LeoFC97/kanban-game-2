\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusao}

We presented a Kanban flow simulator with a Scrum calendar and pairwise synergy, emphasizing \textbf{algorithmic transparency} and \textbf{reproducibility} via a PRNG seed. The paper combines formalization (Section~\ref{sec:modelo}), a ceremony calendar (Section~\ref{sec:ritos}), prototype architecture (Section~\ref{sec:arquitetura}), and a minimal A/B scenario package with the same initial load and global parameters, enabling discussion of flow-metric sensitivity to the synergy matrix.

In essence, the central contribution is not to ``predict'' a real team, but to provide a \textbf{computational laboratory} with a clear boundary between operational hypotheses and empirical evidence. Extensions include calibration on project data, alternative prioritization policies, and integration with human-team play protocols.
