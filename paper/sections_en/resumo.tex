Software teams combine Kanban-style \emph{pull systems} with Scrum cadences, yet explicit quantitative models often omit interpersonal synergy affecting collaboration and handoffs. This paper describes an operational \textbf{discrete-time} simulation model (daily granularity) with flow columns, stochastic per-member capacity, WIP and planning policies, and synergy multipliers for collaborative work and for handoffs between stages, including rework. The engine is reproducible via a PRNG seed. We report a minimal comparative scenario set (A/B) with the same seed and backlog while varying only pairwise synergy, together with flow metrics (per-column counts over time, cycle time, and throughput per sprint). The contribution is methodological: an explicit framework for sensitivity analysis and teaching in operations research, with a clear boundary between operational hypotheses and empirical validation.
